CLIENT PROJECT SCOPE
1.

Client Project Introduction & Client Needs Analysis

Client Project Introduction

Our client is a financial services provider headquartered in Lagos, with branches across the state, that has been operational for over 15 years. The reconciliation and settlement department currently manages customer reconciliation and settlement processes manually. This involves a team of 20-25 staff performing repetitive tasks, leading to delays, inefficiencies, higher operational costs, and customer dissatisfaction. To resolve these challenges, the financial institution seeks to optimize and automate its reconciliation and settlement processes by:

  • Improve productivity by 70%.
  • Reduce human errors by 90%.
  • Enhance customer satisfaction.

Client Needs Analysis

1. Current Process Challenges (AS IS):

  • High manual workload for repetitive tasks
  • Errors caused by human intervention
  • Long processing times

2. Desired State (TO BE):

  • Optimized internal process
  • Automated reconciliation systems requiring little human intervention
  • Tracking and reporting errors in real-time

3. Business Implications:

  • Faster operational turnaround times
  • Improved efficiency and accuracy in settlements
  • Enhanced customer retention

4. Stakeholders:

  • Senior Management
  • Operations and IT departments
  • Customers
  • Financial Regulators
2.

Client Project Scope Definition & Ethics Protocol Requirements

Client Project Scope Definition

In-Scope:

  • Recommend solution
  • Automate reconciliation
  • Train staff
  • Monitor integration
  • Oversee testing and review
  • Monitor deployment

Out-of-Scope:

  • Internal processes are overseen by project manager.
  • Solution development excluded.
  • Unrelated process automation excluded.

Deliverables:

  • Optimized process flow.
  • Framework for automated process.
  • Implementation plan and operational guidelines.
  • Training materials and sessions.

Ethics Protocol Requirements

  • Ensure data confidentiality and compliance.
  • Involve employees to reduce resistance.
  • Communicate job reallocations transparently.
3.

Assumptions, Constraints, and Dependencies

Assumptions

  • Existing IT infrastructure can support automated systems.
  • Staff will adapt to the new system with training.
  • Current manual data is accurate and complete for migration.
  • Budget and resources will be sufficiently allocated.
  • Vendor support for tools is available.

Constraints

  • Project timeline: 100 working days.
  • Budget restrictions within approved limits.
  • Dependence on IT department availability.
  • Adherence to data protection laws.

Dependencies

  • Timely provision of system requirements by the operations team.
  • IT department availability for integration and testing.
  • Senior management approval at key milestones.
  • Vendor support for tools deployment.
  • Effective user training for smooth adoption.
